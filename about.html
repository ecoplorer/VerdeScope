Our Story

Born from a gap
no one else filled.

In January 2025, the NUPRC made the Upstream Petroleum Decarbonisation Template (UPDT) mandatory — embedding GHG compliance into every upstream licence, permit, and Field Development Plan in Nigeria. The enforcement consequence is licence revocation under PIA 2021.

A team of petroleum engineers and climate technologists saw what every operator was about to face: a hard legal obligation with no tools to meet it. Manual documents, spreadsheets, and expensive consultant engagements were the only options — slow, expensive, unverifiable, and not NUPRC-submission-ready.

VerdeScope was incorporated in Lagos in 2026 to build Nigeria's first purpose-built upstream GHG compliance platform — aligned to IPCC Tier 1/2/3, GHG Protocol, ISO 14064-1, and every binding NUPRC regulation — with a carbon market revenue engine attached to every compliance workflow from day one.

The Plan A Benchmark

Our model is directly inspired by Plan A (plana.earth) — Europe's leading carbon management SaaS, which reached EUR 55M exit valuation serving 1,500 enterprise clients in a voluntary EU market. We apply that model to a mandatory enforcement market with a captive, identifiable, first-mover-accessible client base. Same playbook. Structurally superior conditions.

What We Stand For

Our operating principles.

Scientific Rigour

Every calculation in VerdeScope traces to a specific IPCC chapter, an API emission factor, or a GHG Protocol formula. We do not estimate, approximate, or round to suit a narrative. Our platform is a scientific instrument first.

Regulatory Fidelity

We do not interpret Nigeria's regulations for convenience. We implement them exactly as written — every LDAR deadline, every UPDT obligation, every reporting format — because our clients' licences depend on it.

Nigerian Ownership

Nigerian-majority ownership is not just a NOGICD compliance feature — it is a statement of intent. VerdeScope is built by Nigerians for Nigerian industry, with deep understanding of the regulatory context, the commercial culture, and the field realities of the Niger Delta.

Compliance as Profit

We reject the framing of environmental compliance as a cost centre. Every emission source measured is a potential carbon credit. Every methane leak repaired is gas revenue recovered. VerdeScope turns UPDT compliance into a return on investment.
