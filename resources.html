Nigerian Regulatory Instruments

MANDATORY — Jan 2025 Licence Risk

NUPRC Upstream Petroleum Decarbonisation Template (UPDT)

Five obligations mandatory for every licence, permit, and FDP: (1) Decarbonisation strategy in FDP; (2) Time-bound GHG targets; (3) Gas flaring/venting compliance; (4) Methane management programme; (5) CCS/NBS/offsets plan. Non-compliance = revocation under PIA 2021 §96–97.

Authority: NUPRC Effective: January 2025 Legal basis: PIA 2021 §6
UPDT Workflow
In Force — Nov 2022

NUPRC Methane & GHG Emissions Management Guidelines (Guide 0024-2022)

Quarterly GHG reporting (CO₂, CH₄, N₂O) from all production facilities, export terminals, gas processing plants. LDAR quarterly inspection schedules. Equipment-specific controls: pneumatics (5-yr phase-in), compressors, tanks, dehydrators. Tier 1/2/3 methodology. 90-day startup inspection window.

Authority: NUPRC Effective: Nov 2022 71 pages · Guide 0024-2022
LDAR Module
In Force — 2023 2030 Deadline

Gas Flaring, Venting and Methane Emissions (Prevention of Waste and Pollution) Regulations 2023

Zero routine flaring by 2030. Cold venting prohibited (waiver regime applies). 98% Destruction Removal Efficiency (DRE) required on all flares. Quarterly volume reporting. Gas capture and utilisation mandate for all associated gas streams.

Authority: NUPRC Zero flaring deadline: 2030 DRE requirement: 98%

Nigeria NDC 3.0 (Sep 2025) + Carbon Market Framework (Oct 2025)

NDC 3.0 commits Nigeria to 32.2% absolute GHG reduction by 2035; zero flaring by 2030; 60% methane reduction by 2035. The Carbon Market Framework activates the VCM and Article 6.4 mechanism, targeting USD 736M–2.5B market by 2030. Operators who comply with UPDT are automatically positioned for carbon credit origination.

Authority: NCCC / FMEnv Paris Agreement Article 6 USD 2.5B market target

In Force — 2021

Petroleum Industry Act 2021 (PIA 2021) — GHG & Environmental Obligations

Sections 96–97: licence suspension/revocation for environmental non-compliance. Section 102: mandatory GHG management plans in operating licences. Sections 104, 107: gas flaring exemption regime. Section 6: NUPRC mandate to enforce UPDT and emissions standards across the upstream sector.

Authority: Federal Government of Nigeria Enacted: 2021

American Petroleum Institute / ANGA compendium of equipment-count emission factors for pneumatic devices, compressors, storage tanks, and dehydrators. Referenced in NUPRC Guide 0024-2022 as approved Tier 2 methodology.

Required by Nigeria Carbon Market Framework for ETS/carbon tax covered entities. IOC parent companies with EU CSRD obligations. VerdeScope Enterprise includes an IFRS S2 disclosure module aligned to ISSB cross-industry metric requirements.
